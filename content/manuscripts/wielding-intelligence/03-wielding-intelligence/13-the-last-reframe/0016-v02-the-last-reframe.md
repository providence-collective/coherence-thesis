One more thing about the title, before closing this chapter.

Wielding is an old word. It carries the sense of holding something with intention, with skill, with the responsibility that comes from knowing what the thing can do. A sword is wielded. A tool is wielded. Power is wielded. The word does not name a passive relationship. It names an active one, in which the person doing the wielding is changed by what they hold, and the thing held becomes something different in their hands than it would in different hands.

This is what the present moment is asking of humanity. Not whether intelligence will be developed. It is being developed. Not whether it will be deployed at planetary scale. It is being deployed. The question is whether the species can become, in the time available, capable of wielding what it has built without being broken by it. The wielding is a developmental capacity. The capacity has to be cultivated. The cultivation is the work the book has been describing.

This is also why the book is being released now, in the form it is in, with the limitations the next chapter will name directly. The conditions are not waiting. The cultivation cannot be deferred. The wielding is happening, ready or not, and the question is whether enough of the substrate the book has been describing can be built in time to make a difference.

The book is one attempt to contribute to that building. It is not the only attempt, not sufficient on its own, not the answer. It is part of how the people who can already feel the work being asked of them might begin to find each other.

*The next chapter is the honest accounting of what this book is and what it is not. The Final Note that follows is the invitation, as direct as the conditions allow.*
