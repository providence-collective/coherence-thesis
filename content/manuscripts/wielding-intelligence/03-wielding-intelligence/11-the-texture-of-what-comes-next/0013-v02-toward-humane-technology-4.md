*The Final Integration*

The book has now described what humane technology would require. Not as a specification. As a substrate. Coordination infrastructure adequate to civilizational coherence. AI in service of sensemaking. Currencies that perceive what extraction cannot. Economies that circulate value back into the conditions that sustain it. Governance emerging from the quality of the relational field. Builders capable of integration across the worlds modernity kept separate. People doing the inner work that complex coordination actually depends on.

This is what humane technology means in the framing the book has built. It is not a category of product. It is a relationship between technology and the life that produces it. Technologies built within coherent civilizations strengthen coherence. Technologies built within extractive civilizations accelerate extraction. The technologies themselves are downstream of conditions the technology cannot generate.

*This is why the book has spent its length on conditions rather than on specifications. The question of what humane technology would be is, finally, the question of what humanity will become. The Epilogue that follows is not a separate argument. It is the integration of everything the book has been pointing toward, in service of the wielding the title has been naming from the start.*
