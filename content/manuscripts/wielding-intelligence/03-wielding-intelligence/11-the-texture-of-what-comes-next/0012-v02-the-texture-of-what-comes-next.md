The book has been making, across many chapters, a claim worth restating directly in this one. What comes next is not yet visible because it is not yet built. The people who will build it are alive right now and are reading books like this one. The work they will do has begun, in fragments, in many places, at small scales, mostly invisible to the dominant institutional discourse. Whether those fragments coalesce into something coherent enough to constitute the next civilization is the question of the next several decades.

The honest assessment is that the outcome is genuinely undetermined. Civilizations have crossed thresholds like this before, sometimes successfully and sometimes not. The factors that distinguish the successful crossings from the unsuccessful ones include some that cannot be controlled and some that can. What cannot be controlled is whether the underlying conditions—climate, technology, geopolitical pressure—remain within metabolizable range during the period of transition. What can be controlled, partially, is whether the people who could build coherent alternatives find each other in time, whether they develop the inner capacities the work requires, whether they build the relational and economic and governance infrastructure that lets the work proceed at the scale and speed the moment asks for.

This is the work the book has been describing. This is the work it is inviting readers into. The work is not optional for anyone who has read this far and recognized themselves in what the book is pointing toward. The conditions are not waiting. The infrastructure is not being built fast enough. The builders are not finding each other at the density the urgency requires.

What this chapter is asking, finally, is that readers allow themselves to recognize the threshold they are actually in, to grieve what is being lost, to feel what is actually happening in their bodies and in their relationships, and from that more accurate perception to begin doing the work that is theirs to do. Not the work the book prescribes. The work each reader can recognize as theirs once the conditions are seen clearly.

*The book is almost finished. What remains is to synthesize what it has been saying about wielding intelligence, and to make as direct an invitation as the conditions allow.*
