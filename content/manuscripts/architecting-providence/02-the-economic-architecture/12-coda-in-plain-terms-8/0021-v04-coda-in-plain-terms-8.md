Said simply: coherence grows on the timescale of trust and soil, which is far slower than money usually tolerates. You need backers who can wait decades and mean it. — The people who funded the Cardinal Scale were told plainly they might see no financial return for thirty years, and perhaps never in the form they expected. The ones who stayed are the reason it exists. Patient capital is not a nicety here. It is a precondition.


**Toward the coherent substrate.** Capital that demands fast returns is extraction by another name; capital that can wait a generation has, in effect, already changed substrates. The pivot is paced to trust and to soil, not to quarterly impatience — and this chapter is about finding the people who can fund it that way.
