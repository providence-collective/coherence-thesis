Plainly: the resources you need to begin are the same resources that can quietly take you over. The structure of the money matters more than the amount. — The Cardinal Scale's first real grant came with a gentle suggestion about metrics. They rewrote the agreement so that no funder, however generous, could set the community's direction. They got less money and kept their soul. This chapter is how to do that on purpose.


**Toward the coherent substrate.** Extraction's favorite capture is the friendly check. How resources are taken decides whether the pivot survives its own funding or quietly becomes the thing it meant to replace. This is exactly where many well-meant attempts have already failed — and understanding it in advance is how we avoid repeating the same facile motion toward an ideal that never gets executed.
