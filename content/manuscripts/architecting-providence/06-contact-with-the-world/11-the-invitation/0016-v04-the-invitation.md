*The nest was left unfinished on purpose; what remains is the part that was always meant for other hands.*

— The Coherence Thesis, Vol. III

*An invitation is not a promise of success. It is an offer of shared commitment to the attempt.*

This chapter extends an invitation. Not a call to arms. Not a manifesto. Not a claim that the people who accept will be building something that is certain to succeed or that their participation will be recognized by the world in proportion to its importance. An invitation, in the specific sense: an offer to share commitment to work that the preceding chapters have described honestly enough that the people who accept know what they are accepting.

The invitation has a specific addressee. It is not addressed to everyone. The analysis of Chapter Seventeen named who the work requires: people with intellectual seriousness, relational maturity, psychological resilience, and economic realism. Those qualities are not rare, but they are not universal, and the invitation is most usefully accepted by people who have them rather than by people who aspire to them in the abstract. The aspiration is valuable. The work requires the reality.

The invitation is extended to the people who have read through to this chapter — who have followed the argument from Volume I's diagnosis through Volume II's analysis of intelligence without coherence through Volume III's mapping of the design space through Volume IV's architecture of the building. Those people know what they are being invited into. The invitation means something because the knowledge it is based on is real.
