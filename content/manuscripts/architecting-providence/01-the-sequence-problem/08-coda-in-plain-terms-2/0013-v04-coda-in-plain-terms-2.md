In plain terms: do not try to build the network. Build one living community, somewhere real, and let it be real enough to fail. Proof of life comes before proof of scale, always. — The Cardinal Scale's first year was not impressive. Forty people became twenty-six, the shared kitchen flooded, and twice the whole thing nearly dissolved over money. It survived because a handful of people decided that small-and-alive beat large-and-theoretical. That decision is the proof-of-life requirement, lived.


**Toward the coherent substrate.** Extraction never has to prove it is good — only that it is profitable. A coherence-based order earns belief the hard way: by being lived somewhere real, small enough to fail honestly. The pivot has to begin not as an argument but as a place. Coherence becomes the substrate the moment one community actually runs on it.
