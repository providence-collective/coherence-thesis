The plain version: the moment something good starts to grow, gravity pulls it toward becoming extractive — bigger, faster, shallower. Building well means scaling slower than you could. — The Cardinal Scale could have taken in three hundred people in its third year; the interest was there. It took sixty-one. The rule it set itself was almost embarrassingly simple: never grow faster than trust can be built. That sentence is the whole of this chapter, learned the hard way.


**Toward the coherent substrate.** Growth-at-any-cost is the native reflex of extraction; it is how good things get hollowed out. Choosing to scale only as fast as trust can form is the pivot made into a discipline. We do not claim a coherent network must look any one way — only that if it grows like an extractive one, it will become one.
