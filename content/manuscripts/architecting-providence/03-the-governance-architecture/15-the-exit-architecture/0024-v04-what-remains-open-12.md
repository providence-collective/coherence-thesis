The most significant open question about divergence governance concerns how the network maintains constitutional coherence as it grows large enough that not every community can know every other community well. In a small network, constitutional coherence is maintained partly through relational familiarity — communities know each other's practices, have experienced each other's governance, and can evaluate divergence in the context of that familiarity. In a large network, this relational familiarity is not available, and constitutional coherence must be maintained through institutional mechanisms that carry the information relational familiarity previously provided. What those mechanisms are, and whether they can carry the weight of constitutional coherence at planetary scale, is a question the architecture must answer in practice.

*Remember what divergence governance is finally for. It exists so that a community in one watershed and a community in another can each remain wholly themselves — their own culture, their own ecology, their own hard-won way — and still recognize one another as kin in a shared architecture. Not uniformity imposed from a center. Not fragmentation into mutual unintelligibility. The narrow and luminous path between them: coherence without conformity, the many remaining many, and the whole made wiser by each one staying itself.*
