In plain terms: a constitution is not the words on the page — it is what actually happens when someone powerful wants the rules to bend. Build for that day. — The Cardinal Scale's governance was not tested by its good years. It was tested the year a founder wanted an exception just this once. The structure held, the founder did not get the exception, and the community learned that its constitution was real. This chapter is how to build one that holds.


**Toward the coherent substrate.** Coherence decays back into extraction wherever power can quietly concentrate. A constitution that holds under real pressure is how the pivot stays pivoted. We make no claim to have written the final one — only that anyone moving off extraction must design for the bad day, or the bad day will redesign them.
