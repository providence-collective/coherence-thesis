The most consequential open question about the first version concerns the transition to scale. The first version is designed for the first community — a specific community of people who have committed to the architecture and who are motivated to navigate the friction that comes with constitutional design constraints. As the application reaches more participants, it will reach people who are less motivated by constitutional commitment and more motivated by the functional capabilities the application provides. How the application maintains its constitutional character as its participant base diversifies — how it resists the pressure to make technically possible but constitutionally prohibited features available to retain participants who would prefer them — is a governance question that the constitutional specification does not fully answer.

*This is why the first five hundred matter beyond all proportion to their number. They are not a test market on the way to millions. They are the proof that the architecture can hold a living community in coherence through real complexity and real time — the one thing no argument can establish and only a place, somewhere, with real people paying the real price of its principles, can. Everything the wider vision hopes for rests on whether this can be done genuinely, once. That is the weight the first community carries, and the gift it offers everyone who comes after.*
