In plain terms: the first version of the app should be small, should refuse more than it does, and should first of all teach people something about themselves — what presence feels like — before it ever asks them to coordinate. — In the Cardinal Scale the app was a mentor before it was a marketplace. People learned, through its quiet feedback, what a regulated nervous system felt like and what it was to truly meet someone. The coordinating came after the awakening. It always does.


**Toward the coherent substrate.** This is where most people will first *feel* the pivot. The app brings people together on their word and rewards presence instead of harvesting attention — a coordination device, yes, but first a biological mentor that helps a person sense their own state and truly meet another's. It is how the ecosystem begins: people finding out who they really are, how they differ from who they assumed they were, and where — and with whom — they can actually be present. The bodies we can co-regulate with are, more often than not, the ones with whom we land in the feeling of home, or of inspiration. And to be clear: Providence does not begin as a government or a marketplace. It begins as an app, and builds slowly from there.
