The plain version: if this works, the powers it challenges will not fight it head-on — they will adapt, absorb, and offer friendship with strings attached. — The Cardinal Scale's hardest test was not opposition but embrace: the institutions it had diverged from wanted to partner, to fund, to honor it — on terms that would have slowly dissolved it. Surviving success is a discipline of its own, and this chapter is its anatomy.


**Toward the coherent substrate.** Established power rarely fights a coherent alternative head-on; it adapts to absorb it. This is also where the nation-state enters: existing states cannot themselves pivot to a coherent substrate until they can see that smaller sovereign forms — network states, in the language of that work; ICONS, or scales, in ours — actually function and sustain themselves. Surviving power's embrace is how those proofs get the chance to exist.
