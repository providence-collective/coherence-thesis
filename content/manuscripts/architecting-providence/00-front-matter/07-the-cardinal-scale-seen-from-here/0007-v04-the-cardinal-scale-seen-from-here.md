One image to carry from the start, because it is easier to build toward something you can see. Somewhere in the temperate uplands there is a watershed where, a few years on from the moment this writing imagines, eighty-seven people are learning to live by coherence rather than extraction. It is the first community to form fully under this architecture, and so it carries a name in these pages: the Cardinal Scale. It is not a utopia. It has hard winters and harder disagreements, software that does not always work, and people who arrive skeptical and stay that way for a while. What it has that the world outside it has largely lost is a way of keeping faith — with one another, with the land that holds them, with the generations not yet arrived.

We will return to the Cardinal Scale at the close of nearly every chapter, a little at a time, until you can see it whole: how it was funded, how it governs itself, how it kept from being captured, how it survived its own success. We do this because the Cardinal Scale is what the whole argument looks like once it is no longer an argument — a real place, with real people, where the turn out of extraction has actually been made. It is what we are building toward. Everything that follows is how.
