*To be seen accurately — not flattered, not judged, but genuinely seen — is among the rarest experiences a human being can have. It is also, it turns out, the engine of development.*

— The Coherence Thesis, Vol. II

The four streams produce continuous, multimodal data during and after every genuine encounter in the network. The system that integrates them is what we call the Providence Coherence Engine. Its function is twofold: to fuse the four streams into a real-time portrait of the encounter — the minting of the currency — and to return to the participants something of extraordinary value, which is the second and equally important half of the engine's purpose.

The fusion works by what engineers call late integration: each stream is processed independently — the linguistic structure, the vocal autonomics, the cardiac rhythm and its synchrony, the pulse morphology and its endocrine signatures — and the streams are then combined into a single moment-by-moment reading of the encounter's coherence. Where the streams agree, confidence is high. Where they disagree — where the words say one thing and the heart says another — the disagreement is itself information, often the most revealing information of all. The engine maps the full arc of the encounter: where presence was high and where it faltered, where the two participants entered presencing and where they fell out of it, where something genuine occurred and where performance took its place.
