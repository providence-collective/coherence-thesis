Retreats are for people who want to find their people and get clear on their purpose in the company of others. They are curated gatherings organized around shared values and desired impact — not conferences, not workshops, not therapeutic intensives, but something closer to what the best contemplative traditions have always known: that certain kinds of clarity become available only when a carefully chosen group of people moves through a transformational process together.

Rather than the solitary intensity of one-on-one mentorship, the retreat offers a different geometry of encounter. A small number of people, chosen with care, move through a shared process that accelerates clarity, surfaces unexpected collaborations, and creates relationships of a depth that ordinary professional life rarely permits. The retreat serves people who are not yet ready to invest in one-on-one mentorship, or who know they need resonance with others before they can know what individual guidance they require. It is also, in network terms, where the multi-person dynamics of presencing become visible: where the coherence that can emerge between two people is shown to be capable of emerging among many.
