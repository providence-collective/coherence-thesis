- *The dissent, included in good faith:* Credé, M., & Howardson, G. (2017). The structure of group task performance — a second look at "collective intelligence." *Journal of Applied Psychology, 102*(10), 1483–1492; and Bates, T. C., & Gupta, S. (2017). Smart groups of smart people: Evidence for IQ as the origin of collective intelligence. *Intelligence, 60,* 46–56. — Both argue the collective-intelligence factor is weaker than first reported and largely tracks individual ability. We list them because a thesis that hides its strongest critics has not earned trust.
