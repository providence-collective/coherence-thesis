If not Coherence, then what? The honest answer is not one road but three, braided now so tightly they are hard to tell apart.

**The old hard cage: totalitarianism** — the single will, the vanished neighbor, the rewritten past, the boot. We swore *never again,* and did not foresee that its newer cousins would arrive in friendlier clothing, building as ordinary convenience the apparatus the old tyrants could only dream of.

**The new soft manor: neo-feudalism** — what Yanis Varoufakis names *technofeudalism.* You increasingly do not *own;* you *access.* You rent the song, the tool, the seed, the software on your own counter, the rent never ends, and the landlord may revoke it while you sleep. The platform is the manor; the toll is on your very existence; and you are nearer to a serf with a login than a citizen with a deed. The genius of it is that it feels like *convenience* — which is how a toll on existence is meant to feel if it intends to last.

**The watching market: surveillance capitalism** — Shoshana Zuboff's name for the regime that found the inner life still open for claiming. Its raw material is your attention, your hesitations, your three-in-the-morning searches, refined into prediction and sold forward as contracts on your future behavior. You are not the customer. You are the deposit being worked.

The three are not rivals but one convergent tendency: the watching market builds the apparatus, the manor monetizes it, and the hard cage waits, knowing a population already mapped and metered can be *closed* at any time by whoever holds the controls. The infrastructure of soft domination and of hard domination is the *same infrastructure.* It does not care which it serves.
